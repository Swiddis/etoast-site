About Me

Hi, I'm Simeon Widdis (they/them), also known as ExaltedToast, and I'm a distributed systems engineer at AWS. My hobby is thinking about things, such as:

  • Algorithms
  • Baduk
  • Literature
  • Mathematics
  • Pedagogy
  • Philosophy
  • Software

Sometimes, I put those thoughts down in text.

If you want to chat in text, either email me or join ##hackslash on Libera.

Why this site?

In The Pragmatic Programmer, David Thomas and Andy Hunt argue that "Don't Repeat Yourself" isn't about code. It's about life. As a mentor, I've noticed that I repeat myself quite often on various subjects. I wanted a place to deposit some of the ideas that come up a bunch, so I could just link it and call it good. This site is an evolving repository where I share whatever I feel like.

My operating rule of thumb is that if I feel like saying it thrice, it's important enough to make public.

Also, I just happened to have a spare server lying around. May as well make a website with it.

What about the other platforms?

If you're asking this, you're probably thinking of that time I wrote daily posts on LinkedIn. Or alternatively of my other website. The short answer is I didn't feel like continuing either, they weren't me. I wasn't branding them in my style. Rebranding like this is much more my style, and I feel happier this way.

How did you make it?

The pages themselves are just hand-written in HTML, with help from Simple.css. The web server is written in Go, using the Gin Web Framework. Deployment is done through Docker with some additional routing by Nginx.
